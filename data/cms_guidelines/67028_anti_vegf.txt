PROCEDURE: Intravitreal Anti-VEGF Injection
CPT CODE: 67028
SOURCE: CMS LCD L33263 / Payer Clinical Policy

COVERED INDICATIONS

1. Neovascular (Wet) Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD)
   ICD-10: H35.31, H35.32
   - Active choroidal neovascularization (CNV) confirmed by fluorescein angiography (FA) or optical coherence
     tomography angiography (OCTA)
   - OCT demonstrating subretinal fluid, intraretinal fluid, or pigment epithelial detachment attributable to CNV
   - Anti-VEGF agents covered: ranibizumab (Lucentis), aflibercept (Eylea), bevacizumab (Avastin), brolucizumab
     (Beovu), faricimab (Vabysmo)

2. Diabetic Macular Edema (DME)
   ICD-10: E10.3211–E10.3219, E11.3211–E11.3219
   - Center-involving DME confirmed by OCT (central subfield thickness ≥ 250 microns or clinically significant edema)
   - Best-corrected visual acuity of 20/320 or better in the study eye
   - Confirmed diagnosis of diabetes mellitus with documented macular edema on dilated fundus exam

3. Macular Edema Following Retinal Vein Occlusion (RVO)
   ICD-10: H34.811 (central), H34.821 (branch)
   - Macular edema with OCT-confirmed central subfield thickness ≥ 250 microns
   - Visual acuity impairment attributable to macular edema

4. Myopic Choroidal Neovascularization
   ICD-10: H44.20–H44.23
   - Active subfoveal or juxtafoveal CNV in highly myopic eye (≥ –6.00 D or axial length ≥ 26.5 mm)

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

- Baseline best-corrected visual acuity (BCVA) in Snellen or ETDRS letters
- OCT imaging (macular thickness map; central subfield thickness measurement)
- Color fundus photography confirming diagnosis
- Fluorescein angiography or OCTA if CNV diagnosis is uncertain
- Clinical notes documenting indication and absence of contraindications
- For retreatment: interval OCT and VA demonstrating treatment response or need for continued therapy

COVERAGE LIMITATIONS

- Not covered for dry AMD without CNV
- Not covered for stable/inactive CNV without fluid on OCT
- Frequency: up to monthly injections during loading phase; interval per treating physician thereafter
- Bilateral same-day injections require documentation of bilateral active disease
