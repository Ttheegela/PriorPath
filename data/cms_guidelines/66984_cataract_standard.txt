PROCEDURE: Cataract Extraction with Posterior Chamber Intraocular Lens (PCIOL) Implantation
CPT CODE: 66984
SOURCE: CMS NCD 80.8 / Payer Clinical Policy

COVERED INDICATIONS

Cataract surgery (66984) is medically necessary when ALL of the following criteria are met:

1. Visual Acuity Criteria (any one of the following)
   - Best-corrected visual acuity (BCVA) is 20/50 or worse in the operative eye, OR
   - Visual acuity is better than 20/50 but the patient has documented functional impairment that affects
     activities of daily living (driving, reading, occupation), AND the cataract is the primary cause

2. Lens Opacity Documentation
   - Slit-lamp biomicroscopy documenting clinically significant lens opacity (nuclear sclerosis, cortical,
     posterior subcapsular, or combined cataract)
   - Physician documentation that the cataract is the primary cause of visual impairment

3. Pre-operative Requirements
   - Biometry (IOL power calculation) using optical or ultrasound measurement
   - Informed consent documenting risks, benefits, and alternatives discussed
   - Medical clearance if indicated by systemic comorbidities

4. No Contraindications
   - Surgery is not contraindicated by active ocular infection, uncontrolled glaucoma, or systemic instability

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

- Snellen visual acuity (best-corrected, both eyes)
- Slit-lamp examination findings describing lens opacity
- Functional impairment narrative (if VA better than 20/50)
- IOL calculation sheet (keratometry, axial length, target refraction)
- Informed consent signed

COVERAGE LIMITATIONS

- Elective refractive lens exchange for emmetropia/presbyopia is not covered
- Premium IOL upgrade (multifocal, extended depth of focus, toric premium) is patient's responsibility above
  the standard monofocal IOL benefit
- Bilateral same-day surgery (BDSO) covered by some payers with additional documentation
- Second-eye surgery covered when the first eye result is suboptimal or bilateral visually significant cataracts exist
