PROCEDURE: Complex Cataract Surgery
CPT CODE: 66982
SOURCE: CMS LCD / Payer Clinical Policy

COVERED INDICATIONS

Complex cataract surgery (CPT 66982) is covered when BOTH of the following are met:
(1) Standard cataract surgery criteria (see CPT 66984) are satisfied, AND
(2) One or more of the following complicating factors is documented:

COMPLICATING FACTORS (one required)

1. Small Pupil
   - Maximum pharmacologically dilated pupil diameter < 6.0 mm after maximal dilation
   - Requires iris expansion devices (Malyugin ring, iris hooks) or pupilloplasty

2. Dense, Mature, or Brunescent Cataract
   - Nuclear density grade 4+ (LOCS III scale) or white/mature cataract
   - Increased risk of posterior capsule rupture due to reduced red reflex and hard nucleus

3. Pseudoexfoliation (PXF) Syndrome
   - Clinical evidence of pseudoexfoliative material on lens capsule or pupillary margin
   - Associated zonular weakness requiring additional surgical precautions (capsular tension ring, iris hooks)

4. Prior Ocular Surgery
   - Previous corneal transplant (penetrating keratoplasty, DSAEK, DMEK)
   - Prior vitrectomy with silicone oil or gas
   - Prior filtering surgery (trabeculectomy, tube shunt)
   - Prior LASIK/PRK requiring special IOL calculation (Barrett True-K or similar formula)

5. Traumatic Cataract with Structural Damage
   - Ruptured or damaged anterior capsule
   - Subluxated or dislocated lens requiring lensectomy or ACIOL

6. Zonular Weakness / Lens Subluxation
   - Documented zonular dialysis > 90 degrees
   - Marfan syndrome, homocystinuria, or other ectopia lentis conditions
   - Requires capsular tension ring or segment, iris-claw IOL, or scleral fixation

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

- All documentation required for CPT 66984 (see above)
- Specific documentation of the complicating factor (e.g., dilated pupil measurements, slit-lamp photos,
  prior operative reports, genetic diagnosis)
- Operative plan documenting anticipated use of special equipment or technique
