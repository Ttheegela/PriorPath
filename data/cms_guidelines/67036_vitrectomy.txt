PROCEDURE: Pars Plana Vitrectomy (PPV)
CPT CODE: 67036
SOURCE: CMS LCD L35481 / Payer Clinical Policy

COVERED INDICATIONS

1. Vitreous Hemorrhage (Non-Clearing)
   ICD-10: H43.10–H43.13
   - Dense vitreous hemorrhage obscuring posterior segment visualization
   - Hemorrhage persisting > 4 weeks without spontaneous clearing (or > 1–2 weeks in diabetic patients)
   - B-scan ultrasound confirming vitreous hemorrhage and ruling out retinal detachment

2. Tractional Retinal Detachment (TRD) — Diabetic
   ICD-10: H33.40–H33.43
   - Fibrovascular proliferation causing tractional detachment threatening or involving the macula
   - OCT or fundus exam documenting detachment with traction
   - Combined traction-rhegmatogenous detachment is covered without waiting period

3. Epiretinal Membrane (ERM)
   ICD-10: H35.371–H35.373
   - Visually significant ERM with BCVA 20/50 or worse in the operative eye, OR
   - ERM causing metamorphopsia that significantly impacts quality of life
   - OCT documentation of membrane with retinal distortion or thickening

4. Macular Hole (Full-Thickness)
   ICD-10: H35.341–H35.343
   - Stage 2, 3, or 4 full-thickness macular hole on OCT
   - Minimum diameter ≥ 250 microns; base diameter documented on OCT
   - Stage 1 (impending) not covered without functional loss

5. Retained Lens Fragments Post-Cataract Surgery
   ICD-10: H59.021–H59.023
   - Any retained cortical or nuclear material post-phacoemulsification
   - Associated with elevated IOP, inflammation, or corneal decompensation

6. Rhegmatogenous Retinal Detachment (RRD) with Vitrectomy Component
   ICD-10: H33.001–H33.009
   - Complex RRD requiring scleral buckling + vitrectomy or vitrectomy alone

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

- Color fundus photography and/or fundus fluorescein angiography
- OCT imaging (for ERM, macular hole, TRD)
- B-scan ultrasound (for vitreous hemorrhage obscuring view)
- Visual acuity (BCVA)
- Clinical examination findings documenting the indication
- Operative plan

COVERAGE LIMITATIONS

- Elective vitrectomy for floaters (uncomplicated) is generally not covered
- ERM with VA better than 20/40 and no metamorphopsia requires additional documentation of functional impact
